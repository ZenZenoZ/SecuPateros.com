Signs of a Secure Website


Look for the S in HTTPS

     Many URLs begin with “https” instead of http, this shows that they are secure because they have an SSL certificate. This protects information entered into the site, whereas websites with http are easily accessible, and are the information is more easily stolen.

Check for a Website Privacy Policy

     Many websites have one and this demonstrates that this website is aware and actively complying with laws that provide security.

Find Their Contact Information

     If the website provides contact information, such as an email or social media account, it does not guarantee security, but they demonstrate that the website can provide you with assistance if need be.

Verify Their Trust Seal

     If you see a sign like “verified” or “secure” it is likely a trust seal, which indicates the website has a security partner. This can also indicate that the website scans for malware. To make sure it is authentic if you click the badge then it should take you to a verification page.

Know the Signs of Malware

Unusual Behavior

     If your device is suddenly slower, freezes or crashes more often, or shows unexpected error messages, it might be infected with malware.

Unwanted Ads or Redirects

     Frequent, pop-up ads or your browser redirecting to unfamiliar websites can be signs of adware or other types of malware.

Fake Antivirus Programs

     Be wary of antivirus programs that appear out of nowhere, especially if they claim to have found issues on your device and ask for payment to fix them. These are often scareware programs.

Social Media Spam

     If you notice fake posts or direct messages on your social media accounts that contain inflammatory statements or links, be cautious as these could be attempts to spread malware.

Malicious redirects

     If you type in a URL and are redirected to another site especially one that looks suspicious – you have been affected by a malicious redirect. They are often used in conjunction with phishing kits.

Ransomware Threats

     Ransomware will often encrypt your files and demand payment to restore access. Some types may also lock you out of your device entirely.

Disabled System Tools

     If you suddenly can't access system tools like Task Manager or Registry Editor, it might be due to malware trying to hide its presence.

Silent Activity

     Some malware tries to stay hidden, only becoming active when it receives instructions from a command and control server. This could be for activities like participating in a DDoS attack or stealing your personal information.

To Prevent Malware

Keep Your Software Up to Date

     Regularly update your operating system, web browser, and other software to ensure that any known security vulnerabilities are addressed.

Use Antivirus and Antimalware Software

     Install and use reputable antivirus and antimalware software to protect your computer from malware.

Be cautious when browsing the web

     Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unverified software. Be especially cautious when visiting unfamiliar websites.

Use strong and unique passwords

     Use complex and unique passwords for all of your accounts, and consider using a password manager to generate and store them.

Limit administrative access

     Limit the number of users who have administrative access to your computer and website.

Implement two-factor authentication (2FA)

     Implement 2FA for all of your accounts to add an extra layer of security.

Backup your data

     Regularly backup your data to ensure that you can quickly restore it in the event of a malware attack.

Use a web application firewall

     Use a WAF to help protect your website from common web-based attacks, such as SQL injection and cross-site scripting (XSS).

Monitor your website's logs

     Regularly review your website's logs to detect any unusual activity, such as failed login attempts or suspicious file modifications.

Use reputable security plugins

     Consider using security plugins that can help detect and prevent malware infections.

Be cautious with third-party code

     Be careful when adding third-party code, such as JavaScript or iframes, to your website. Make sure that the code is from a reputable source and that it is regularly updated.

Educate yourself and your team

     Regularly educate yourself and your team on the latest website security threats and best practices. This can help prevent accidental downloads of malware or other security incidents.

About the Campaign:

   Our campaign aims to raise cybersecurity awareness in Pateros City, a vibrant and growing community in the Philippines. We believe that cybersecurity is a critical issue that affects everyone, and we want to help Pateros City residents stay safe online.



Our campaign has three main goals

Education

We want to provide Pateros City residents with the knowledge and skills they need to protect themselves online. We will offer a series of workshops, webinars, and other educational resources that cover topics such as password management, phishing prevention, and safe browsing practices.

Awareness

We want to raise awareness about the importance of cybersecurity in Pateros City. We will work with local organizations, schools, and businesses to promote cybersecurity best practices and to encourage residents to take steps to protect themselves online.

Community

We want to build a strong and supportive cybersecurity community in Pateros City. We will create opportunities for residents to connect, share ideas, and learn from each other. We will also work to establish partnerships with local organizations and businesses to ensure that our campaign has a lasting impact.
